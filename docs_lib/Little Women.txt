Little Women
By Louisa May Alcott
Chapter 1
Playing Pilgrims
Christmas wont be Christmas without any presents grumbled Jo lying on the rug
It is so dreadful to be poor sighed Meg looking down at her old dress
I dont think its fair for some girls to have plenty of pretty things and other girls nothing at all added little Amy with an injured sniff
Weve got father and mother and each other said Beth contentedly from her corner
The four young faces on which the firelight shone brightened at the cheerful words but darkened again as Jo said sadly
We havent got father and shall not have him for a long time
She did not say perhaps never but each silently added the thought remembering how far he had gone to fight in the war
Nobody spoke for a minute then Meg said in an altered tone
You know the reason mother proposed not having any presents this Christmas was because it is going to be a hard winter for everyone and she thinks we ought not to spend money for pleasure when our men are suffering so in the army We cant do much but we can make our little sacrifices and ought to do it gladly But I am afraid I dont
The truth is we shall all have to wait for our fun and our pretty things said Jo I am tired of waiting I want new books new gloves new ribbons and new everything
I wanted to buy some new drawing paper sighed Amy mine is all worn and smudged
I thought we might each have a dollar to spend as we liked said Beth softly I wanted to buy new sheet music for my piano
Well we have no dollars to spare said Meg Mother says we must save every cent to send supplies to father and the soldiers Our small comforts can wait until the war ends
I was thinking what we might give mother instead of receiving gifts said Jo suddenly She works so hard for us all and never buys anything for herself We could each chip in a little to get her something nice
That is a lovely idea cried Meg What shall we get her
I wish I could buy her a warm shawl said Meg hers is thin and worn out
I would get her a new book of poems said Jo she loves reading so much
I want to buy her a pair of soft gloves whispered Beth
I will get her a small bottle of nice cologne offered Amy she likes gentle scents
We can put our money together and pick one good thing or small separate gifts said Jo We each have a little saved from our small chores and errands
Just then the clock struck six and Beth rose to set a chair by the fire and place mothers slippers near the hearth to warm them
How cold those slippers look said Meg mother must be tired and chilled when she comes home
The girls fell silent watching the fire and thinking of their absent father far away from home
Soon light footsteps sounded outside the door and the four girls ran to greet their mother a gentle tired woman with a kind worn face
She sat down wrapped herself in her thin shawl and smiled at her daughters
Have you all had a pleasant day she asked
We talked about Christmas and planned small gifts for you mother answered Meg
Marmee looked surprised and her eyes softened with affection
You need not spend your savings on me my dears I only wish for you to be happy this holiday season
But we want to give you something to show our love said Jo we miss father and we want to take care of you as you take care of us
The mother held out her arms and all four girls crowded close to her warm embrace
I have a story to read to you tonight before bed she said a story about pilgrims who traveled through hard times with kindness and patience It reminds me of our own little family journey through these difficult days
The girls gathered around the fire ready to listen forgetting their small disappointments about missing holiday presents their hearts full of quiet love for one another and their mother
